Finding a Downed Plane in Open Water

Mathematical Competition in Modeling - 2015
Authors: Elizabeth Dye, John McAvey, Connor Bell

PDF

Transoceanic fight has become a regular occurrence; there are as many as 770 transatlantic fights aloneper day (1). However, when a aircraft flies over an ocean it becomes signi cantly more dicult to track by radar. For this reason, it is dicult to find aircraft that have lost contact with their respective control towers. Finding downed aircraft quickly is vital to the safety of passengers and faith of the consumers. This paper explores techniques to minimize the search space and increase the chance of success for rescue teams.
